The Surat-Tapi District Co-operative Milk Producers’ Union Ltd. (SUMUL), formerly known as The Surat 
District Co-operative Milk Producers’ Union Ltd., is the project proponent (PP) and implementing agency 
for the rainwater harvesting and borewell recharge initiative across its member dairies. SUMUL is a 
district-level cooperative milk producers’ union operating under the Gujarat Co-operative Milk Marketing 
Federation Ltd. (GCMMF), the marketers of Amul brand dairy products. 

In line with its long-standing 
objective of ensuring sustainable rural development and resource management, SUMUL has undertaken 
the installation of rainwater harvesting systems at its affiliated dairy units. At present, approximately 100 
dairies under SUMUL have implemented rainwater harvesting infrastructure, wherein harvested 
rainwater is scientifically channeled for borewell recharge to enhance groundwater availability and long
term water security for dairy operations. 
____________________
The project has been implemented across 100 dairy society locations distributed within Surat and Tapi 
districts of Gujarat, covering a wide range of talukas including Songadh, Mandavi, Umarpada, Mahuva, 
Valod, Bardoli, Vyara, Nizer, Kukarmunda, Kamrej, Mangrol, Olpad, Uchchal, and Dolvan. These locations 
represent both tribal and non-tribal regions, varying hydrogeological conditions, and differing levels of 
groundwater stress. 

The selected sites correspond to functional Dairy Cooperative Societies (DCS) operating under The Surat
Tapi District Co-operative Milk Producers’ Union Ltd. (SUMUL). Each site has been geo-referenced using 
latitude and longitude coordinates to ensure spatial traceability, facilitate monitoring, and support 
verification of the installed recharge structures. 

The distribution of project locations demonstrates a decentralized implementation strategy, with 
installations spread across interior rural villages, semi-urban dairy clusters, and hilly tribal belts. This 
geographic spread allows the project to address groundwater recharge needs across diverse 
physiographic settings while ensuring equitable coverage within the operational jurisdiction of SUMUL.
